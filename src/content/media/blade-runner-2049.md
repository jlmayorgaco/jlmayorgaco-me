Sequels shouldn't be this good. Villeneuve respects the original while expanding the world in meaningful ways. The visual language - orange Vegas, blue LA, Wallace's amber-lit headquarters - is stunning.

Ryan Gosling's K is the perfect protagonist for this story. A replicant searching for meaning, for identity, for something real. His journey from obedient blade runner to someone who chooses sacrifice is beautifully done.

Roger Deakins' cinematography deserved that Oscar. Every frame could be a painting. The hologram scene with Joi and Mariette is technically brilliant and emotionally complex.

**Why I rewatch:** The pacing. It's slow, meditative, philosophical. In an age of quick cuts and exposition dumps, 2049 lets you sit with the world.
