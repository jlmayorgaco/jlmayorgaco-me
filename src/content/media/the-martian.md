The ultimate engineering movie. "I'm going to have to science the shit out of this" should be the motto of every R&D lab. What makes this work is that the science is mostly real - orbital mechanics, growing potatoes in Martian soil, the hab systems.

Matt Damon carries the film through sheer optimism. The scene where he calculates he needs 600 sols of food and just... starts working on it. No panic, no despair, just problem-solving. That's the engineering mindset.

The supporting cast is stacked (Chiwetel Ejiofor, Jessica Chastain, Jeff Daniels) and everyone gets a moment. The Hermes crew's decision at the end is earned through the whole film.

**Why engineers love it:** It's competence porn. Watching someone methodically solve impossible problems with duct tape and orbital mechanics.
