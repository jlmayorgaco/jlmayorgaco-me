A masterclass in character development. Walter White's transformation from mild-mannered chemistry teacher to drug kingpin is so gradual you don't notice when you start rooting for the villain. Bryan Cranston deserved every Emmy.

The show treats chemistry with respect - the blue meth, the ricin, the fulminated mercury. It's not just a plot device, it's integrated into Walt's character. "I am the one who knocks" is iconic for a reason.

The cinematography of New Mexico becomes a character itself. The wide shots of the desert, the harsh lighting, everything emphasizes isolation and desolation. Every season builds on the last without losing focus.

**Still think about:** The "Ozymandias" episode. Television doesn't get better than that.
